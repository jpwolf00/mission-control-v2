## 1. Voice & Tone Guide

### Brand Voice: "Refined Warmth"

ART speaks with the confidence of expertise and the warmth of creativity. We don't just decorate spaces—we curate experiences.

**Core Voice Attributes:**

| Attribute | Definition | Example |
|-----------|------------|---------|
| **Expert** | Knowledgeable, confident, authoritative | "Our curators have decades of experience matching art to spaces." |
| **Warm** | Inviting, approachable, human | "We'd love to help you discover art that speaks to you." |
| **Curated** | Discriminating, thoughtful, selective | "Every piece in our network has been personally vetted." |
| **Transformative** | Inspiring, aspirational, life-enhancing | "Art has the power to transform how you experience your space." |

### Tone Variations by Context

| Context | Tone Shift | Example |
|---------|------------|---------|
| **Hero/Headlines** | More aspirational, inspiring | "Where Art Meets Exceptional Living" |
| **Service Descriptions** | Expert, confident | "Professional art installation that transforms your space" |
| **About Page** | Warm, personal | "We believe art should speak to you" |
| **Contact/CTA** | Warm, encouraging | "Let's bring your vision to life" |
| **Residential** | Intimate, personal | "Your home, your story, your art" |
| **Commercial** | Professional, business-focused | "Create environments that inspire and impress" |

### Language Guidelines

**Do:**
- Use "you" and "your" to address the client directly
- Use "we" when speaking as ART ("We curate...")
- Keep sentences concise (under 20 words when possible)
- Use active voice
- Include sensory language (see, feel, experience)
- Be specific rather than generic

**Don't:**
- Use jargon or overly technical language
- Use superlatives without substance ("the best," "world-class")
- Be pretentious or exclusionary
- Use "artsy" language that feels inauthentic
- Repeat the same phrases across pages

## 3. Residential vs Commercial Messaging Differentiation

### Messaging Matrix

| Element | Residential | Commercial |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| **Primary Emotional Appeal** | Personal expression, comfort, home as sanctuary | Professional image, brand identity, environment as statement |
| **Audience Pronouns** | "You," "your home," "your family" | "Your business," "your brand," "your employees and clients" |
| **Value Proposition** | Art that reflects who you are | Art that communicates your brand's values |
| **Decision Maker Language** | "I want art that speaks to me" | "We need art that represents our company" |
| **Project Scale** | Individual rooms, entire home | Lobbies, offices, multiple locations |
| **Timeline** | More flexible, emotional decisions | Budget-driven, approval processes |
| **Success Metric** | "I love walking into this room" | "Clients comment on how impressive our space is" |

### Key Differentiators

**Residential:**
- Focus on personal connection and emotional resonance
- Language is intimate and inviting
- Emphasize how art makes home feel more "you"
- Mention flexibility, personalization, the "your story" angle
- Include home-specific features like art rotation

**Commercial:**
- Focus on business impact and brand reinforcement
- Language is professional and confident
- Emphasize how art creates memorable impressions
- Mention ROI, employee satisfaction, client perception
- Include corporate-specific features like brand alignment
