## 1. Voice & Tone Guide

### Brand Essence
- **Premium but approachable** — luxury art consulting without snobbery
- **Expert guidance** — confident, knowledgeable without being pedantic
- **Collaborative** — "we" language, partnership with client
- **Inspiring** — paint possibilities, not just list services

### Voice Attributes

| Attribute | Description | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Confident** | Clear recommendations, no hedging | "We transform spaces..." vs "We try to transform..." |
| **Warm** | Welcoming, human, not corporate | "We'd love to hear from you" |
| **Professional** | Expertise shines through | "curated network of artists" |
| **Visionary** | Paint the outcome | "personal gallery you'll love coming home to" |

### Tone Spectrum

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| Hero headlines | Inspirational, bold | "Where Art Meets Exceptional Living" |
| Body copy | Warm, informative | Explains services with benefits |
| CTAs | Direct, encouraging | "Schedule Consultation" |
| Contact form | Friendly, low-friction | "Let's create something beautiful" |

### Key Phrases (Brand Vocabulary)

**Use These:**
- "curated" / "curation" — conveys intentionality, expertise
- "transform" — powerful outcome-focused word
- "thoughtful" — implies care and consideration
- "bespoke" / "personalized" — signals custom approach
- "bring to life" — dynamic, action-oriented

**Avoid:**
- "artwork" (use "art" or "pieces" in most contexts)
- "client" (use "homeowners", "businesses", or "you")
- Jargon (no "art advisory" — say "art consulting")
- Passive voice

### Grammar & Style

- Use Oxford comma
- Write headings in title case (not all caps)
- Keep paragraphs short (2-3 sentences max)
- Use bullet points for lists of benefits/features
- Include location (NYC) where relevant
